Literature Review
Guideline Only • 1 min
ACOG CO 797, 2020
ACOG Committee Opinion No. 797 — Prevention of Group B Streptococcal Early-Onset Disease in Newborns. American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists, 2020.
In patients with a high-risk penicillin allergy (urticaria, angioedema, anaphylaxis), clindamycin should be used when the isolate is susceptible. When susceptibility data are unavailable, vancomycin is the recommended alternative. Susceptibility testing of GBS isolates is therefore essential in the management of penicillin-allergic patients.
Dosing Guidance
ACOG / SMFM Guidance — Updated Vancomycin Dosing for GBS Prophylaxis.
Weight-based dosing of 20 mg/kg IV q8h (max 3 g/dose) is recommended over the older flat-dose 1 g q12h regimen, as it achieves superior maternal and fetal tissue concentrations. Pharmacy collaboration is essential for appropriate dosing in labor.

Modify prenatal GBS order set to automatically include susceptibility testing when penicillin allergy is documented — no separate action required by provider.

Fully digitize laboratory send-out requests. Remove dependency on paper forms that can be separated from specimens during transport.

Standardize a prenatal visit checklist verifying that susceptibility results are received and documented for all GBS+ patients with documented antibiotic allergy.

07
Take-Home Points
For Discussion
🔬

Susceptibility testing is mandatory in penicillin-allergic patients

Without it, we default to vancomycin — a broader-spectrum agent associated with greater side effects and inferior fetal tissue levels.

⚖️

Vancomycin dosing matters

Weight-based dosing (20 mg/kg q8h) is the current standard. Older flat-dose 1 g q12h protocols should be retired.

🔉

System failures are not individual failures

The missing send-out form was a process gap — not a single provider error. Solutions must be system-level.

💡

The intervention window is prenatal, not intrapartum

Catching a missing result at the labor admission is too late. The 36-week visit is our last safe opportunity to verify.

Regimen Dose Interval Notes
Older flat-dose 1 g IV q12h Subtherapeutic fetal levels; no longer recommended
ACOG 2020 (current) 20 mg/kg IV q8h Max 3 g/dose. Achieves adequate maternal & fetal tissue levels.
Infusion duration Over 60 min (min). Longer for doses >1.5 g Faster infusion → Red man syndrome risk
Pharmacy coordination Trough / AUC monitoring optional in short labor Critical to calculate actual weight-based dose at admission
WHY IT MATTERS
Inadequate vancomycin levels leave neonates at risk. Weight-based dosing corrects for the pharmacokinetic variability inherent in pregnancy.
ACTION ITEM
Review current labor floor vancomycin order set with pharmacy. Update default dose to weight-based if not already done.

  • Q: Why wasn't the lab result reviewed before labor?

    Postpartum endometritis is multifactorial. However, inadequate GBS prophylaxis may have contributed. The case represents a system failure that placed this patient at elevated risk.
