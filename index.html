Literature Review
Guidelines Only • 1 min
ACOG CO 797, 2020
American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists. Committee Opinion No. 797. 2020.
In patients with a high-risk penicillin allergy (urticaria, angioedema, anaphylaxis), clindamycin is preferred when the isolate is susceptible. When susceptibility data are unavailable, vancomycin is the recommended alternative. Susceptibility testing is therefore mandatory in penicillin-allergic patients — it is the pivot point that determines antibiotic choice.
Onwuchuruba et al., AJOG 2014
Onwuchuruba CN, Towers CV, Howard BC, et al. Transplacental passage of vancomycin from mother to neonate. Am J Obstet Gynecol. 2014;210:352.e1–4.
Standard-dose vancomycin (1g IV): only 32% of maternal and 9% of neonatal blood samples reached therapeutic range at delivery. Higher-dose (weight-based): 83% of both maternal and neonatal samples were therapeutic. Current ACOG guidance: 20 mg/kg IV q8–12h, max 2 g/dose. The Cerner OB default of 1g q12h does not meet this standard.
09
Quality Improvement Actions
System-Level Solutions

🔍 Complete the Lab Investigation

Determine the exact mechanism of lab failure. Did the Cerner field text not transmit? Was it ignored? Implement corrective action with lab leadership and document for quality reporting.

📋 Redesign the EMR Order Set

Modify prenatal GBS order set to automatically trigger susceptibility testing when penicillin allergy is documented — eliminate reliance on a free-text field that may be truncated or misinterpreted.

🔔 Automated Missing-Result Alert

Build EMR alert: "GBS positive — susceptibilities absent" triggered when allergy flag is present. Alert fires at result receipt and at the next prenatal encounter closest to term.

✅ 36-Week Prenatal Checklist

Standardize a prenatal visit checklist confirming susceptibility results are received and charted for all GBS+ patients with any documented antibiotic allergy — before the patient can reach the labor floor.

10
Take-Home Points
For Discussion
🔬

Susceptibility testing is essential in PCN-allergic patients

Without it, we default to vancomycin. When the isolate is sensitive, clindamycin is preferred — and that information requires a susceptibility result.

⚖️

Vancomycin dosing: weight-based > flat-dose

20 mg/kg q8h achieves 83% neonatal therapeutic levels vs 9% with the old 1g q12h. Cerner's OB default should be updated — this is an action item for pharmacy and OB leadership.

🔊

System failures require system solutions

The order was placed correctly. The failure was downstream in the lab. Pointing at individuals derails learning — we need structural fixes: alerts, auto-orders, checklists.

💡

The intervention window is prenatal — not at the OBER

Discovering a missing susceptibility at labor admission is too late. The 36-week prenatal visit is the last safe opportunity to find and fix this gap.

WHY IT MATTERS
Vancomycin transplacental passage is concentration-dependent. A flat 1g dose — especially with only one dose given — may not achieve bactericidal fetal levels against GBS.
ACTION ITEM
Cerner OB order set currently defaults to 1g q12h. This should be updated to weight-based 20 mg/kg q8h with pharmacy collaboration.
